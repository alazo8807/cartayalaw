About Cartaya Law


At Cartaya Law, we understand that knowledge is power. This is why we will take the extra time to walk you through the legal intricacies of your case, providing you with high standard legal advice and a general understanding of legal proceedings.

While we always encourage our clients to explore settlements and out-of-court options (which are typically more cost-effective) we will always advance the clients’ best interest and will fight relentlessly in or out of court.

There are no wrong questions and we are here to answer them all. We want our clients to feel comfortable with each step they take, so we follow the rule that well-informed clients are better equipped to make the right decisions. We understand how stressful and emotionally-taxing legal proceedings can be, so we practice compassion and empathy, and will always be willing to hear you out.

About


Jorge A. Cartaya graduated from the University of Toronto in June 2011 with an Honours Bachelor of Arts with High Distinction. Mr. Cartaya then attended Osgoode Hall Law School at York University where he obtained his Juris Doctor (JD) degree in 2015.

Mr. Cartaya completed the Law Practice Program (LPP) at Ryerson University while working for Mazinani Law Offices until May 2016. During his time at Mazinani Law Offices, he worked in civil litigation, family law, criminal law, and immigration-related matters. Mr. Cartaya was officially called to the bar in June 2016 as Barrister And Solicitor.

In June 2016, Mr. Cartaya started working with Blue Letter Law Professional Corporation as an associate lawyer. At Blue Letter Law, he worked on numerous family and wills/estates matters. In January 2018 Mr. Cartaya opened his own practice, Cartaya Law Office.

Mr. Cartaya has appeared before the Ontario Court of Justice and the Superior Court of Justice on multiple occasions. He has negotiated settlements with opposing counsels while litigating matters requiring a more aggressive approach. He has dealt with complex family cases and is a results-driven practitioner. While he encourages his clients to explore settlement or out-of-court options which are often more cost-effective, Mr. Cartaya has successfully litigated many family law proceedings at court, where he has relentlessly and skillfully fought for his clients’ interests.

Mr. Cartaya fluently speaks and writes Spanish, and is a member of the Toronto Lawyers Association. He accepts Legal Aid as well as private retainers.
